# Financial Definitions

## Revenue
Total money a company earns from selling its products or services
before any costs are subtracted.

## Revenue CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
The steady annual growth rate that would take revenue from its
starting value to its ending value over a number of years.
Formula: (Ending Revenue / Starting Revenue) ^ (1 / Years) - 1

## EPS (Earnings Per Share)
Net income divided by total shares outstanding.
Represents how much profit the company made per share.

## Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Operating Cash Flow minus Capital Expenditures.
The actual cash left over after running the business and investing
in equipment. Harder to manipulate than reported earnings.

## Interest Coverage Ratio
EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes) divided by Interest Expense.
Measures how easily a company can pay its interest obligations.
Above 8x is generally considered healthy.

## Leverage
Total Debt divided by EBITDA.
Measures how much debt the company carries relative to its earnings.
Below 2x is generally conservative. Above 4x warrants caution.
